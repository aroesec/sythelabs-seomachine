## 3. Key Statistics to Include

### Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- $446.1 million in real estate-specific BEC losses (2022) - [FBI IC3](https://www.ic3.gov/PSA/2024/PSA240911)
- BEC losses 7x higher than ALL ransomware losses across ALL industries
- 1,760% year-over-year increase in BEC attacks since generative AI popularization
- $24,586 average BEC wire transfer request (Q1 2025)
- 40% of BEC phishing emails now AI-generated

### Ransomware
- 61% of CRE ransomware attacks successfully compromise backups
- $2.73 million average ransomware recovery cost (excluding ransom)
- Only 28% of organizations recover within one week (2024)

### Industry Vulnerability Stats
- 61% of real estate firms globally have no dedicated cybersecurity team (Deloitte 2024)
- Only 27% perform regular vulnerability assessments or penetration testing
- 38% year-over-year increase in ransomware attacks against real estate (2024)
- Only 9% of CRE organizations have quantified their cyber exposure
- 40% of CRE companies lack accurate asset inventory
- 50%+ of breaches initiated through phishing/social engineering
- Nearly 50% of breaches begin with compromised credentials

### Wire Fraud
- $145 million in reported real estate wire fraud losses (2024)
- 93% of title/escrow companies experienced phishing attacks (past 12 months)
- 17% of title companies sent client money to fraudulent accounts
- $70,000 median loss for financial fraud victims in real estate

### Smart Building/IoT
- Fewer than 50% of organizations continuously monitor IoT/OT environments
- 38% of CRE respondents reported financial losses from cyberattacks
- 36% cited reputational damage as operational impact
- 31% experienced loss of customer/partner relationships

- High-value transactions create attractive targets

- Financial records and payment information

- Proprietary property and lease data

### Industry Maturity Gap
- 61% have no dedicated security team

- Only 9% have quantified cyber exposure

## The Top Cybersecurity Threats Facing CRE in 2025 (600-700 words)
### Business Email Compromise: The $446 Million Threat
- Why BEC exceeds ransomware losses in real estate
- How attacks work (email compromise → wire fraud)
- AI-powered phishing making attacks more convincing
- Statistics: 1,760% increase since AI tools emerged

### Ransomware Targeting Property Operations
- 61% of attacks compromise backups
- Average $2.73M recovery cost
- Extended recovery times disrupting operations
- Real examples: property management system lockouts

### Smart Building and IoT Vulnerabilities
- BMS as new attack surface
- Legacy protocols (BACnet, Modbus) without encryption
- HVAC, access control, elevator systems at risk
- Cyber-physical consequences (tenant lockouts, safety systems)

### Supply Chain and Third-Party Risks
- Contractor and vendor access to networks
- Weaker security controls in partners
- Service provider breaches affecting multiple properties

## Building Management Systems: The Overlooked Attack Surface (400-500 words)
### Why BMS Security Matters
- Integration with IT networks creating exposure
- Legacy systems lacking security updates
- Multiple access points (web interfaces, wireless, vendor connections)

### Common BMS Vulnerabilities
- Outdated software and unpatched systems
- Default credentials and weak access controls
- Insecure protocols lacking encryption
- Inadequate network segmentation

### Real-World Impact Scenarios
- HVAC manipulation affecting tenant operations
- Access control compromise enabling physical access
- Fire suppression system interference
- Elevator control disruption

### Direct Financial Losses
- Wire fraud losses (median $70,000)

### Indirect Costs
- Reputational damage (36% report impact)
